Affordable Connectivity Program

Tech Goes Home (TGH) is a nonprofit dedicated to addressing the digital inequities that pose a significant barrier to opportunity and success for thousands of students, workers, and families across Massachusetts. We bring computers, internet, and training to those without so students can do homework, adults can find jobs and manage finances, seniors can connect with loved ones, and all can lead healthier lives.

In the past five years alone, 21,000 learners have graduated from TGH programs, and we have distributed more than 15,000 new computers in communities across Eastern Massachusetts. The coronavirus pandemic along with the growing national consciousness of systematic racism has substantially increased awareness of the digital inequities that low-income households face as many of our school, employment, healthcare and civic programs have moved to virtual platforms.

The Opportunity

The Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) Manager at Tech Goes Home (TGH) is a critical role that helps to advance the organization's mission of closing the digital divide.

Reporting to the Chief Advocacy Officer (CAO), the ACP Manager will work to identify and collaborate with key partners and community members to expand access to the federally-funded ACP benefit program, which helps households afford the broadband they need for work, school, healthcare, and more. The Manager will lead the development and implementation of strategies to build capacity within the community to sustain and exceed trends of outreach and enrollment. The ideal candidate will have strong leadership and analytical skills, as well as the ability to communicate effectively with a variety of stakeholders.

As an Advocacy Team member, the ACP Manager will work with the team to address the challenges digital inequity creates for people in Massachusetts and beyond. The Manager will collaborate with CAO and TGH team members to cultivate a culture for continuous learning and an understanding of the barriers facing our partners, learners and instructors. The Manager’s portfolio will inform the local, state, and federal fields of digital equity internet adoption and assist to position the organization as a local and national thought leader. The Manager will serve as a resource in Advocacy to provide in-depth data and impact analysis of engaging, advancing, and sustaining relationships to further the TGH ACP expansion work.

Relationship Management
  • Develop and Lead a team of ACP Navigators, providing guidance, support, and supervision to ensure the team's success in assisting TGH learners, instructors, and graduates.
  • Lead community engagement prospecting activities and cultivate relationships through introductory calls, video conferencing, and in-person meetings and events.
  • Collaborate with CAO and the Advocacy department to develop strategies and goals for the ACP Navigator team, aligning with TGH's overall internet adoption objectives.
  • Monitor the team's performance, including number of outreach calls and texts, enrollment outcomes, and take proactive measures to ensure high performance.
  • Conduct regular team meetings to communicate updates, provide training and coaching, and address any challenges or concerns.
Operations
  • Collaborate with TGH's communications and marketing to develop outreach materials and strategies that effectively promote the ACP to the community.
  • Maintain awareness of the Federal ACP guidelines, its eligibility criteria, internet plans that support the program - serve as the ACP subject matter expert.
  • Identify and secure quantitative and qualitative metrics to evaluate the success and impact ACP activities.
  • Track engagement and activity flow with various stakeholders.
Strategy
  • Develop, in collaboration with the Chief Advocacy Officer, a comprehensive strategic vision and implementation process to scale ACP activities and enrollments.
  • Analyze and project goals to enroll TGH learners and instructors in ACP benefits.
  • Create and establish ACP benchmarks, timelines, and resources to achieve strategic goals.
  • Research and remain current on innovative systems and models for partnership excellence as the organization grows.
Competencies and abilities
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills, with the ability to communicate effectively with individuals from diverse educational and cultural backgrounds.
  • Exceptional customer service skills, with the ability to establish trust, provide guidance, and address concerns sensitively and professionally.
  • Strong problem-solving and conflict-resolution abilities, with the capacity to handle stressful situations and find creative solutions.
  • Cultural competency and sensitivity, with the ability to work with individuals from various backgrounds and foster an inclusive and supportive environment.
  • Strong organizational skills and attention to detail, ensuring accurate record-keeping and reporting.
  • Previous experience in relevant community outreach or similar roles is desirable.
  • Ability to demonstrate a positive attitude, excellent interpersonal skills, growth mindset, and a sense of humor in working with diverse TGH learners, coworkers, and the community.
Experience and Expectations

While no candidate is likely to demonstrate every ideal experience, successful applicants will submit a cover letter that responds to most if not all of the following expectations with clear and convincing examples during previous employment.

  • A solid commitment to the mission of TGH, as well as a passion for building a more just, equitable community and helping people improve their lives.
  • The ability to work collaboratively and respectfully with people of different races, cultures, socioeconomic backgrounds, educational levels, and perspectives is necessary.
  • Previous professional work experience, with a strong preference for candidates who have launched or, at minimum, coordinated impactful work.
  • Excellent organizational/writing skills and solid executive functioning to juggle multiple priorities, details, and deadlines with grace and timeliness.
  • Confident, engaging, empathetic speaker in both one-on-one and group settings.
  • Experience working with a high-performance, collaborative, constructive peer group and navigating the challenging conversations that may be necessary to do so.
  • Lived experience with the challenges of digital equity is valuable and valued at Tech Goes Home.
  • Represent TGH to individuals and institutions interested in its work, online, on the phone, and in person.
  • A sense of humor goes a long way in a small, hardworking nonprofit. We value that!
Compensation

TGH offers competitive compensation for all staff positions. The exact salary that will be offered to the candidate will be determined based on the successful candidate’s skills and experience.

Salary will be commensurate with all relevant experience, professional and otherwise, and the range for this position is $69,900-$85,000. Comprehensive benefits include health, dental, and vision insurance, flexible spending accounts, paid parental leave, retirement savings, vacation, STD/LTD/Life Insurance, and paid professional development.

At this time, all TGH staff are working hybrid remotely, with assistance for the resources needed to do so. With consideration and proof of vaccination, employees currently work a flexible on-site schedule each week. Many aspects of this specific role require in-person gatherings and meetings. This position requires the candidate to be in our Boston office at least twice weekly. TGH is in an attractive shared workspace close to Back Bay Station. Candidates from the Greater Boston area are preferred.
